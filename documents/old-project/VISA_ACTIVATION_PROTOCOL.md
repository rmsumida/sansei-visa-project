# Visa Activation Protocol — Remote Residency

> **Last Updated:** 2026-03-24 (SESSION-000)
> **Status:** NOT ACTIVE — Depends on COE approval (Phase 2).

**Purpose:** Define the exact sequence for the single activation trip to Japan that converts the COE into a live visa, secures long-term re-entry rights, and establishes the tax firewall — all while maintaining permanent residence in Texas.

---

## Pre-Trip Requirements

Before booking any travel:

- [ ] Certificate of Eligibility (COE) received and in hand.
- [ ] Confirm COE issuance date — entry must occur within **3 months** of this date.
- [ ] Valid US passport (confirm expiration date provides adequate buffer).
- [ ] Confirm which Japanese port of entry issues Zairyu Cards on arrival (most major international airports do — Narita, Haneda, Kansai).
- [ ] Identify the nearest regional Immigration Bureau to the registered address for the re-entry permit application.
- [ ] Identify the municipal city hall for address registration and Tenshutsu-todoke.

---

## Activation Sequence

### Step 1: Entry & Zairyu Card

- **Action:** Arrive at a Japanese international airport.
- **Window:** Within 3 months of COE issuance.
- **Result:** Receive the Zairyu Card (在留カード) at the immigration port of entry.
- **Duration:** Same day as arrival.

### Step 2: Address Registration

- **Action:** Visit the municipal city hall (市区町村役場) and register a residential address.
- **Purpose:** Finalizes the Zairyu Card. The address is printed on the card.
- **Address Options:** Can be temporary — hotel, friend's address, or other arrangement. It will be deregistered in Step 4.
- **Duration:** ~1 day (same day if city hall is nearby).

### Step 3: Multiple Re-entry Permit

- **Action:** Visit the regional Immigration Bureau (入国管理局) and apply for the **Multiple Re-entry Permit** (数次再入国許可).
- **Validity:** Up to 5 years.
- **Why:** Bypasses the limited 1-year "Special Re-entry Permit" that is automatically granted at the airport. The Multiple Re-entry Permit legally protects visa status during extended residence abroad.
- **Duration:** ~1 day (may require a return visit to pick up the permit).

### Step 4: Tax Firewall — Tenshutsu-todoke (転出届)

- **Action:** Return to the same city hall from Step 2 and file a **Moving Out Notice** (転出届).
- **Purpose:** Formally notifies the municipality that the visa holder is establishing non-resident tax status.
- **Effect:** Prevents accumulation of:
  - National Health Insurance (国民健康保険) premiums
  - National Pension (国民年金) liabilities
- **Timing:** MUST be filed **before** departing Japan. Non-negotiable.
- **Duration:** Same day.

### Step 5: Departure

- **Action:** Depart Japan.
- **Result:** Activation complete. Visa is live. Re-entry permit is active. Tax status is clean.

This is a conservative 7-day estimate. If city hall and Immigration Bureau are in the same city and processing is fast, the trip could be compressed to 4–5 days.

---

## Critical Warnings

1. **COE Expiration:** If you do not enter Japan within 3 months of COE issuance, it expires and you must re-apply from scratch.
2. **Tenshutsu-todoke Timing:** If you depart without filing this notice, NHI and pension bills will accumulate. These are enforceable debts.
3. **Re-entry Permit vs. Special Re-entry:** The airport "Special Re-entry Permit" is only valid for 1 year and is granted automatically. It is NOT sufficient for the remote residency model. You MUST apply for the full Multiple Re-entry Permit at the Immigration Bureau.
4. **Address Registration Order:** You cannot file Tenshutsu-todoke without first having a registered address. Steps 2 and 4 must be at the same city hall.
