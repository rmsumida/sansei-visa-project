### DEC-002: 4-phase project structure
- **Status:** 🔒 Locked
- **Date:** 2026-03-26
- **Session:** 001
- **Decision:** Organize the project into 4 sequential phases: (1) Lineage Evidence Retrieval, (2) COE Application, (3) Visa Activation Trip, (4) Visa Maintenance
- **Alternatives considered:** Single flat task list, milestone-based tracking
- **Rationale:** Each phase has distinct prerequisites and dependencies. Sequential structure ensures no phase begins before its prerequisites are met, and makes blockers visible.
- **Conditions for reopening:** If a fundamental change in visa requirements eliminates or merges phases

### DEC-003: Yakura line is hot-standby only
- **Status:** 🔒 Locked
- **Date:** 2026-03-26
- **Session:** 001
- **Decision:** Do not actively pursue the Yakura (maternal) lineage unless the Sumida line fails at Koseki retrieval
- **Alternatives considered:** Pursue both lines in parallel for redundancy
- **Rationale:** The Sumida line has stronger confirmed evidence (exact municipality, kanji, mother's name). Pursuing both lines doubles document costs and effort. The Yakura line remains available via cousin if needed.
- **Conditions for reopening:** Koseki Tohon retrieval fails or returns incomplete for the Sumida line

### DEC-005: File Tenshutsu-todoke during activation trip
- **Status:** 🔒 Locked
- **Date:** 2026-03-26
- **Session:** 001
- **Decision:** File a Moving Out Notice (転出届) before departing Japan on the activation trip to establish non-resident tax status
- **Alternatives considered:** Maintain Japanese resident status for potential benefits
- **Rationale:** Prevents accumulation of National Health Insurance premiums and National Pension liabilities. Ryan's primary residence is Texas — maintaining Japanese resident status creates unnecessary financial obligations.
- **Conditions for reopening:** If maintaining resident status provides a material benefit (e.g., needed for GK operations or property ownership)
